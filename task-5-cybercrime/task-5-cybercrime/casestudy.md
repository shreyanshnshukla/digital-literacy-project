Cyber crime Case Study: Phishing Attacks

Phishing is a type of cyber crime which tricks an individual to reveal private information like password, banking details and OTP's. In most cases, an individual may receive an email from an impersonated source that looks authentic and legitimate, perhaps from your bank or favorite service. These mails generally claim to have some issue with your account and asks you to verify its authenticity as soon as possible.

The email then has a link directing to a fake and misleading website which is visually the replica of the genuine web page. When you key in the username and password or bank details into this website the credentials are stolen and used in unauthorized transactions and to impersonate you.

It is young people or students who are often targeted as they spend a significant amount of time using the internet and many are unaware or have lack of knowledge to detect such mails. Students may then suffer financial damages and also loss of important accounts.

This case study stresses the importance of vigilance when it comes to email, web pages and social sites.
