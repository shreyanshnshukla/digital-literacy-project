Cyber Crime Case study: Phishing Attack.
A Phishing attack is a cyber crime which refers to those type of attacks in which the perpetrators impersonate themselves as a trustworthy entity and lure the victim to give up their personal information, for example- their login credentials (passwords, OTPs), or their bank account details etc. A typical situation can be when a student gets an email, in which, the bank or some renowned service provider informs him about some problem in his account and wants him to get the details of his account verified quickly. It has a link which leads to a dummy website. When the student gives his login details or bank account details, the fraud gets the login details and uses them to carry out unlawful activities or identity theft.
The young ones or the students are easy targets because they mostly use the online media and might not doubt the nature of emails and hence this kind of activity can really cause harm to a person, which may lead to financial problems and loss of confidential data of the victim and sometimes loss of access to critical accounts.
